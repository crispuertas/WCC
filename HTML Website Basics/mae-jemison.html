Mae Jemison

"Don't let anyone rob you of your imagination, your creativity, or your curiosity. It's your place in the world; it's your life. Go on and do all you can with it, and make it the life you want to live."

Mae C. Jemison is the first African-American female astronaut. In 1992, she flew into space aboard the Endeavour, becoming the first African-American woman in space.

Interesting Facts

  1. Essence Science and Technology Award - 1988
  2. Gamma Sigma Gamma Woman of the Year - 1990
  3. Ebony Black Achievement Award - 1992
  4. Montgomery Fellowship from Dartmouth College - 1993
